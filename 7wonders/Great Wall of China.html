GREAT WALL OF CHINA - China

Introduction:

The Great Wall of China is a series of fortifications made of stone, brick, tamped earth, wood, and other materials. Built along the northern borders of China, it was designed to protect Chinese states and empires against invasions and raids.

Overview / History:

Construction began as early as the 7th century BC, with most well-known sections built during the Ming Dynasty (1368–1644). The Wall stretches over 21,000 kilometers across northern China.

Architecture & Design:

The Wall is built from various materials, depending on the region. Watchtowers, barracks, and signal towers are key features, offering both defense and communication capabilities.

Cultural Significance:

The Great Wall symbolizes China's strength and perseverance. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most recognizable landmarks in the world.

Things to do:

  • Hike popular sections like Badaling and Mutianyu.
  • Enjoy panoramic views of mountains and valleys.
  • Explore ancient watchtowers and fortresses.
  • Visit the Wall at sunset for stunning scenery.

How to reach:

Nearest major airport: Beijing Capital International Airport.
From Beijing, tour buses and trains can take you to Wall sections like Badaling and Mutianyu. Taxis and private tours are also available.

Tourist Information:

Location: Northern China, across several provinces

Best time to visit: March to May and September to November

Entry fee: Varies by section (typically 40–60 CNY)

Nearby attractions: Beijing, Ming Tombs, Summer Palace

Book Now

Visitor Tips:

Wear comfortable shoes, carry water, avoid crowded sections during holidays, and check weather before hiking. Some areas are steep and uneven.
