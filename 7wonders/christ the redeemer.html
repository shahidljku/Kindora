CHRIST THE REDEEMER - Rio de Janeiro, Brazil

Introduction:

Christ the Redeemer is an iconic Art Deco statue of Jesus Christ located in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. It stands atop the Corcovado mountain and is one of the most recognized religious monuments in the world.

Overview / History:

Completed in 1931, the statue was designed by French sculptor Paul Landowski and built by Brazilian engineer Heitor da Silva Costa. It stands 30 meters (98 feet) tall with a 28-meter (92 feet) arm span.

Architecture & Design:

Constructed from reinforced concrete and covered with soapstone, the statue showcases the Art Deco style. Its outstretched arms symbolize peace and embrace the city of Rio below.

Cultural Significance:

Christ the Redeemer is a symbol of Christianity around the world and a cultural icon of Brazil. It attracts millions of visitors every year and is listed as one of the New Seven Wonders of the World.

Things to do:

  • Ride the Corcovado Train through Tijuca Forest to reach the top.
  • Enjoy panoramic views of Rio de Janeiro.
  • Visit during sunrise or sunset for beautiful lighting.
  • Explore nearby attractions like Sugarloaf Mountain and Copacabana.

How to reach:

Nearest airport: Rio de Janeiro–Galeão International Airport.
Take a train from Cosme Velho station or drive up via Paineiras Road. Shuttle vans are also available from various city points.

Tourist Information:

Location: Corcovado Mountain, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil

Best time to visit: May to October (dry season)

Entry fee: Includes train or shuttle fare to summit (varies by option)

Nearby attractions: Sugarloaf Mountain, Ipanema Beach, Botanical Garden

Book Now

Visitor Tips:

Buy tickets in advance, bring sunglasses and sunscreen, and prepare for crowds. Early morning or weekdays are best for a quieter experience.
