Mount Fuji - Japan

Introduction:

Mount Fuji is Japan’s tallest peak at 3,776 meters and an active stratovolcano. Located about 100 km southwest of Tokyo, it is one of Japan’s most iconic landmarks.

Overview / History:

Mount Fuji has long been a symbol of Japan and a pilgrimage site. The current form of the volcano was shaped over centuries of eruptions, the last major one being in 1707.

Architecture & Design:

While a natural wonder, Mount Fuji is surrounded by cultural shrines, climbing routes, and the famous Fuji Five Lakes. The symmetrical volcanic cone is celebrated in art and literature worldwide.

Cultural Significance:

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Mount Fuji is deeply tied to Japanese culture, spirituality, and national identity. It is revered as a sacred mountain.

Things to Do:

  • Climb Mount Fuji during official climbing season (July–September).
  • Visit the Fuji Five Lakes region for scenic views.
  • Enjoy hot springs (onsen) around Hakone and Kawaguchiko.
  • See Mount Fuji reflected in Lake Kawaguchi.

How to Reach:

Nearest airport: Tokyo Haneda or Narita International Airport.
From Tokyo, take a train or bus to Kawaguchiko or Gotemba for access to Mount Fuji.

Tourist Information:

Location: Honshu Island, Japan

Best time to visit: July to September (climbing) or winter for views

Entry fee: Free (voluntary climbing donation recommended)

Nearby attractions: Fuji Five Lakes, Hakone, Chureito Pagoda

Book Now

Visitor Tips:

Weather can change quickly, so bring proper gear. Stay overnight at mountain huts if climbing. For the best views, plan around clear days.
