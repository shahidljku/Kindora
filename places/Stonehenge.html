Stonehenge - England, UK

Introduction:

Stonehenge is a prehistoric monument located in Wiltshire, England. This world-famous site consists of a ring of standing stones and is regarded as one of the most important cultural landmarks of the UK.

Overview / History:

Archaeologists believe Stonehenge was constructed from 3000 BC to 2000 BC. Its exact purpose remains a mystery, though it is thought to have been used for ceremonial or astronomical purposes.

Architecture & Design:

The monument features large sarsen stones and smaller bluestones arranged in a circular layout. The engineering and transportation of these massive stones over long distances remain a remarkable feat.

Cultural Significance:

Stonehenge is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and symbolizes Britain's ancient history and mystery. It attracts millions of visitors each year, especially during solstice events.

Things to Do:

  • Walk around the stone circle and visitor trails.
  • Visit the Stonehenge Visitor Centre and exhibitions.
  • Learn about Neolithic life in recreated houses.
  • Attend the summer or winter solstice gatherings.

How to Reach:

Nearest airport: London Heathrow or Bristol Airport.
From London, take a train to Salisbury, then a bus or taxi to Stonehenge.

Tourist Information:

Location: Wiltshire, England, UK

Best time to visit: Late spring to early autumn

Entry fee: Around £25 for adults

Nearby attractions: Salisbury Cathedral, Avebury, Old Sarum

Book Now

Visitor Tips:

Book tickets online in advance. Dress warmly and be prepared for windy weather. The stones cannot be touched during regular visits, but special tours allow closer access.
