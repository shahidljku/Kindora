Statue of Liberty - New York, USA

Introduction:

The Statue of Liberty, gifted by France in 1886, is one of the most iconic symbols of freedom and democracy. Located on Liberty Island in New York Harbor, it welcomes millions of visitors each year.

Overview / History:

Designed by French sculptor Frédéric Auguste Bartholdi and built by Gustave Eiffel, the statue commemorates the centennial of American independence. It became a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1984.

Architecture & Design:

Standing at 93 meters (305 feet) including its pedestal, the statue is made of copper sheets over a steel framework. The torch represents enlightenment, while the broken chains at her feet symbolize freedom.

Cultural Significance:

The Statue of Liberty is a universal symbol of freedom, democracy, and hope for immigrants arriving in the United States. It remains one of the most visited landmarks in America.

Things to Do:

  • Take a ferry to Liberty Island and Ellis Island.
  • Climb to the crown for panoramic views of Manhattan.
  • Visit the Statue of Liberty Museum.
  • Explore nearby Battery Park in Lower Manhattan.

How to Reach:

Nearest airport: John F. Kennedy International Airport (JFK) or Newark Liberty International Airport.
Ferries depart from Battery Park (NYC) and Liberty State Park (NJ).

Tourist Information:

Location: Liberty Island, New York Harbor, USA

Best time to visit: April to October

Entry fee: Ferry tickets range from $24–30 (extra for pedestal/crown access)

Nearby attractions: Ellis Island, Battery Park, One World Trade Center

Book Now

Visitor Tips:

Book ferry tickets in advance, especially for crown access. Arrive early to avoid long queues. Bring water and sunscreen during summer visits.
