EIFFEL TOWER - Paris, France

Introduction:

The Eiffel Tower is one of the most recognizable landmarks in the world, located in Paris, France. Built in 1889 for the Exposition Universelle (World's Fair), it has become a global cultural icon of France and a top tourist attraction.

Overview / History:

Designed by engineer Gustave Eiffel, the tower was initially criticized but soon became a beloved symbol. Standing at 330 meters, it was the tallest structure in the world until 1930.

Architecture & Design:

The Eiffel Tower is made of wrought iron and features three levels accessible to visitors. Its lattice structure is both functional and aesthetic, making it one of the most impressive engineering feats of its time.

Cultural Significance:

Beyond being a tourist attraction, the tower is a symbol of romance, innovation, and French culture. Millions of people visit it every year.

Things to do:

  • Take an elevator or climb stairs to observation decks.
  • Enjoy panoramic views of Paris.
  • Dine at restaurants inside the tower.
  • See the Eiffel Tower illuminated at night.

How to reach:

Nearest airport: Charles de Gaulle International Airport.
Accessible by Paris Metro (Bir-Hakeim station) or RER train (Champ de Mars-Tour Eiffel station).

Tourist Information:

Location: Paris, France

Best time to visit: April to June, September to November

Entry fee: €10 – €26 depending on level access

Nearby attractions: Louvre Museum, Seine River Cruise, Arc de Triomphe

Book Now

Visitor Tips:

Book tickets online to avoid queues, visit early morning or evening for fewer crowds, and don’t miss the sparkling light show after sunset.
